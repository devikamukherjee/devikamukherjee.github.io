Peer-reviewed publication at the ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems — the flagship venue for HCI research — to be presented in Barcelona in 2026.

CHI 2026 · Barcelona First author ACM Digital Library

Belt and Whistles: Adding Lower Body Collision Awareness for MR Experiences

Mukherjee, D., Abdlkarim, D., Giunchi, D., Di Luca, M., Ofek, E. (2026)
Proceedings of the ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI 2026), Barcelona, Spain.

A wearable haptic belt that extends mixed-reality collision awareness below the user's field of view — addressing a known safety gap in immersive experiences where lower-body obstacles fall outside visual attention. Controlled user studies (n=35+) compared multisensory feedback strategies for embodiment, task performance, and perceived safety.

IoT · Machine Learning · Product design
Hydrino — IoT Hydration Tracking
An IoT hydration-tracking product pairing an Arduino Nano sensing unit with a Flutter app and an ML pipeline that learns water-bottle usage patterns to nudge healthier habits.
ArduinoFlutter Machine LearningIoT
Expand details

The problem. Daily hydration is one of the lowest-friction health behaviours to track in principle — and one of the easiest to forget in practice. Existing trackers depend on manual logging that doesn't survive a busy day.

The approach. An Arduino Nano embedded in a water-bottle base senses use events (lifts, pours) and streams them to a companion Flutter application. A lightweight ML model learns each user's baseline pattern over the first week, then surfaces nudges only when behaviour deviates — avoiding notification fatigue.

What I led. End-to-end product design, from sensor selection through Flutter UI and the ML feature pipeline. Early user feedback shaped the move from explicit goal-setting to learned baselines.
